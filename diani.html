About Diani Beach

Diani Beach, located 30 kilometers south of Mombasa on Kenya's Indian Ocean coastline, consistently ranks among Africa's best beaches. Stretching for 17 kilometers, this pristine paradise features powdery white coral sand, crystal-clear turquoise waters protected by coral reefs, and swaying palm trees that create the quintessential tropical beach experience.

Beyond its postcard-perfect beaches, Diani offers a diverse ecosystem. The ancient Kaya Kinondo sacred forest, just inland from the beach, protects rare coastal forest and represents the spiritual heritage of the Digo people. The coral reefs teeming with marine life attract snorkelers and divers from around the world, while the warm waters host dolphins, sea turtles, and during migration seasons, even whale sharks and humpback whales.

Diani seamlessly blends relaxation with adventure. The beach itself invites long walks, sunset watching, and lounging under palm trees, while the surrounding area offers kite surfing, skydiving, deep-sea fishing, and visits to the Colobus Conservation Center protecting endangered monkeys. The blend of Swahili culture, fresh seafood, and warm hospitality makes Diani the perfect coastal retreat after a safari adventure.
