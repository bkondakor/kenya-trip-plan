About Masai Mara

The Masai Mara National Reserve is Kenya's most celebrated safari destination, covering 1,510 square kilometers of pristine savannah in southwestern Kenya. Named after the ancestral inhabitants, the Maasai people, and the Mara River which cuts through it, this reserve is globally renowned for hosting the spectacular Great Migration between July and October.

Home to over 95 species of mammals and 570 bird species, the Mara offers unparalleled wildlife viewing year-round. The reserve boasts exceptional concentrations of lions, cheetahs, and leopards, making it one of the best places in Africa to see big cats. The endless golden plains, dotted with acacia trees and crossed by the life-giving Mara River, create the quintessential African landscape.

Beyond wildlife, the Mara offers authentic cultural encounters with Maasai communities who have coexisted with wildlife for centuries. Their traditional pastoral lifestyle and distinctive red shukas add vibrant cultural richness to any safari experience.
