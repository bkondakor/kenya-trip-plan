About Hell's Gate National Park

Hell's Gate National Park is one of Kenya's most unique and adventurous destinations. Named for the intense geothermal activity that bubbles beneath its surface, this 68-square-kilometer park in the Great Rift Valley is the only Kenyan national park where visitors can walk, cycle, and rock climb freely among wildlife without the confines of a vehicle.

The park's dramatic landscape of towering red cliffs, volcanic rock formations, and geothermal steam vents inspired the scenery in Disney's "The Lion King." The two massive red-tinged cliffs forming the "Hell's Gate" gorge rise dramatically from the valley floor, while Fischer's Tower and Central Tower provide world-class rock climbing routes that have challenged climbers since the 1930s.

Despite lacking large predators, Hell's Gate harbors abundant wildlife including buffaloes, zebras, elands, hartebeests, Thomson's gazelles, and over 100 bird species including Verreaux's eagles and augur buzzards nesting on the cliffs. The narrow Hell's Gate Gorge, with its towering walls and naturally-heated springs, offers an unforgettable hiking experience through water-carved passages barely three meters wide. The park also hosts Kenya's first geothermal power station, harnessing the volcanic energy that gives the park its evocative name.

üìÖ Best Time to Visit

Year-round destination, June - March for cycling and hiking

Excellent year-round. Dry seasons (June-October, January-March) offer easiest cycling conditions. Early mornings provide best wildlife viewing and cooler temperatures for hiking. Avoid gorge during heavy rains due to flash flood risk.
