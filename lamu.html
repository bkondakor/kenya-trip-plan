About Lamu Archipelago

Lamu Archipelago, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, represents the oldest continuously inhabited Swahili settlement in Kenya at over 700 years old. Lamu Town remains gloriously car-free - only donkeys and dhows navigate the narrow stone streets lined with coral buildings featuring intricately carved wooden doors with brass studs.

The architecture fuses Swahili, Arabic, Persian, and Indian influences into a living heritage site where residents still practice traditional crafts: dhow building in Matondoni village, elaborate furniture carving, basket weaving, and calligraphy.
