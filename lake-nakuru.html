About Lake Nakuru National Park

Lake Nakuru National Park, nestled in the Great Rift Valley, is one of Kenya's most spectacular bird sanctuaries and rhino reserves. Covering 188 square kilometers, the park surrounds the shallow, alkaline Lake Nakuru, which has become world-famous for its stunning display of millions of flamingos that create a spectacular pink fringe along the shoreline.

Beyond the flamingos, Lake Nakuru is recognized as one of Africa's finest rhino sanctuaries. The park hosts both black and white rhinos, carefully protected in a fully fenced sanctuary. In fact, Lake Nakuru has one of the highest rhino densities in Africa, offering visitors exceptional opportunities to see both species in their natural habitat. The park's successful conservation program has made it a model for rhino protection across the continent.

The park's diverse habitats include rocky cliffs, acacia forests, euphorbia forests, and the lake itself, supporting over 450 bird species and a rich variety of wildlife including lions, leopards, Rothschild's giraffes, waterbucks, and buffaloes. The stunning viewpoints from Baboon Cliff and Lion Hill offer breathtaking panoramas of the lake and the surrounding Rift Valley escarpment.

üìÖ Best Time to Visit

Year-round destination, June - March for peak flamingo numbers

Flamingo populations fluctuate with water levels and algae availability. Rhino viewing is excellent year-round. Early mornings offer best wildlife and bird watching opportunities.
