About Lake Naivasha

Lake Naivasha is a stunning freshwater lake nestled in the Great Rift Valley at 1,884 meters elevation, just 90 kilometers northwest of Nairobi. Unlike many Rift Valley lakes, Naivasha's fresh water supports a completely different ecosystem from its alkaline neighbors, creating a verdant oasis teeming with wildlife and birdlife. The name derives from the Maasai "Nai'posha," meaning "rough water," referring to sudden storms that can whip up waves on the otherwise tranquil surface.

The lake's size fluctuates dramatically with rainfall - expanding from 114 to 991 square kilometers over its history - creating a dynamic ecosystem that supports over 400 bird species, making it one of Kenya's premier birding destinations. The papyrus-fringed shores harbor one of East Africa's largest hippo populations, while the clear waters support abundant fish including black bass, tilapia, and crayfish introduced for commercial fishing.

What sets Lake Naivasha apart is Crescent Island, a private sanctuary where visitors can walk freely among zebras, wildebeests, giraffes, and waterbucks without fences or vehicles - an extraordinarily rare experience in Kenya. The lake's proximity to Nairobi, combined with the adjacent Hell's Gate National Park, makes it an ideal weekend escape or stopover on longer safaris. The surrounding flower farms produce much of Europe's cut flowers, adding colorful splashes to the lakeside landscape.

üìÖ Best Time to Visit

Year-round destination, November - March for peak bird migration

Pleasant year-round climate. November-March brings European migrant birds joining resident species. June-September offers clearer skies and comfortable temperatures. Morning boat rides provide calmest water and best wildlife viewing.

Boat Safari

Crescent Island Walking Safari

Walk freely among zebras, giraffes, wildebeests, and waterbucks on this private sanctuary peninsula. No fences, no vehicles - just you and the wildlife.

Bird Watching Extraordinaire

Spot over 400 bird species including African fish eagles, pelicans, cormorants, herons, kingfishers, and seasonal migrants from Europe.
